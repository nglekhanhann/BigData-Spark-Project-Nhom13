# Big Data Product Sales Analytics with PySpark

## Overview

This project analyzes a large product sales dataset using Hadoop, HDFS, PySpark, Spark SQL, and Spark MLlib. The dataset contains 200,000 sales transactions from 2023–2024, covering product categories, regions, states, revenue, profit, quantity, and pricing information.

The main goal of this project is to identify business patterns across product categories, regions, price segments, and monthly performance, then translate the findings into practical recommendations for sales and profit improvement.

## Objectives

* Process and analyze large-scale sales data using PySpark and Spark SQL.
* Explore revenue, profit, profit margin, product category, region, and state-level performance.
* Identify high-revenue products, low-margin risks, regional business patterns, and seasonal trends.
* Apply machine learning techniques to support customer or product segmentation and sales prediction.
* Present analytical findings through a structured report and presentation.

## Tools & Technologies

* Hadoop / HDFS
* Apache Spark
* PySpark
* Spark SQL
* Spark MLlib
* Python
* PowerPoint / Word

## Key Analysis Areas

* Revenue and profit performance by category and sub-category
* Regional and state-level sales performance
* Monthly sales trends and seasonality
* Price segment analysis
* Product performance classification
* Profit margin diagnosis
* K-Means clustering
* Random Forest regression

## Key Insights

* Electronics generated the highest revenue share but showed a lower profit margin compared with several other categories.
* Home & Furniture contributed strongly to revenue and profit, especially in specific regions.
* Some high-revenue products required margin monitoring because strong sales did not always translate into high profitability.
* Regional analysis showed different category strengths across East, West, South, and Centre regions.
* Price segment analysis suggested that premium products did not always produce the strongest margin performance.
* The product action grid helped classify products into groups such as scale-up, monitor, and improvement priorities.
